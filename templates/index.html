Sign Language Translator enables the hearing impaired user to communicate efficiently in sign language, and the application will translate the same into text/speech. The user has to train the model, by recording its own sign language gestures. Internally it uses MobileNet and KNN classifier to classify the gestures.

A sign language translator project aims to bridge communication gaps between hearing-impaired individuals and the hearing world. It typically involves using computer vision and machine learning to interpret sign language gestures captured by a camera and convert them into spoken or written language. This technology has the potential to enhance accessibility and inclusivity for the deaf and hard of hearing community, making everyday interactions easier. Some popular implementations include devices or mobile apps that provide real-time sign language interpretation, empowering individuals with hearing impairments to communicate more effectively.
